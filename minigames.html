Splomb

Characters by Luke L. UI by Aaron L. 3D models by Brock J. Music by Connor G.

Made over the course of one weekend in November 2017, Splomb showcases how much I have grown as a game developer over the past two years. It was made at the London Game Jam (#LoJam), in conjunction with three artists. I was the sole programmer of the group. Together, we came up with the idea of this game, scoped it for a weekend-long development, and finished it before the deadline.

Most impressively, NO external assets were used. Every piece of code, every art asset, even the music, it was all created that weekend.

The game is a two-player "couch co-op" game. The goal is to cover as much area as possible with your colour of paint.

The version linked here uses mouse and keyboard controls: Player 1 moves with W-A-S-D and drops paint bombs with E. Player 2 moves with Directional Arrows and drops paint bombs with Space. The "Boss Bomb" in the middle of the arena gives a large advantage to whoever touches it first. For the showcase section of the Game Jam, we also developed a 4-player game mode that uses X-box controllers.

XMY Game

XMY Game (not final title) has been in development for the past two years. For details on the project development / difficulties / learning experiences, please see the Projects page.

The game is a socializing simulator crossed with Deck / Card mechanics. You pick a person at the start to befriend, and then play the game to try to befriend them. To that end, you need to develop relationships with other characters, who can then introduce you to your target.

The card mechanics help you express your own personality. Want to be a snarky guest? Fill your deck with "Sarcasm" cards. Want to be an attentive listener? Add in those "Empathic" and "Sincere" cards.

Note: the playable demo is NOT the full game. Your dialogue options are limited, your personality deck is pre-made, and you cannot complete the game. On the other hand, there are some fully fleshed out dialogue trees to give you an idea of what the final product will be like.

Thorn Breaker

One of my earlier games. I followed online instructions and tutorials to build this clone of Arkanoid or brick-breaker. My original contribution to the tried-and-true Arkanoid formula include turning the arena sideways and my (unfortunate) art decisions.

To play the game, click the primary mouse button to launch the ball, and move the mouse to control the launch pad.
