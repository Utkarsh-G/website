Professional Experience

The following is a list of my professional engineering experiences in reverse chronological order. If you want the story-mode version, then read from the bottom up.

Game Developer Mar 2015 – Present

Self-Employed, Mississauga, Ontario

  • Developed multiple games using C# and Unity Editor
  • Rigorously followed Test-Driven Development (TDD) via Unity Test Tools
  • Coded enemy AI and card game mechanics
  • Developed website to showcase projects

It started as a fun little six-month long project to make a game. By the end of the first six months, my code-base had spiralled out of control into a fragile incomprehensible mess, and I realized I didn't have the animation skills to make the game a reality on my own. So I started afresh on a new project: much less focus on animation / music / anything I couldn't create myself, and far more focused on coding style, cleanliness, and robustness. I am now a total convert to the cult of Test Driven Development. TDD + Clean coding techniques have turned my work into a pleasant romp. Adding feature or refactoring is so much quicker with clean, readable code, and it is entirely painless with a healthy set of unit-tests present as safety nets. I've also worked on smaller games along the way just to learn more about Unity and C#. Some of them are showcased on this site.

Applications Engineer Nov 2013 – Dec 2014

Calnex Solutions, Linlithgow, Scotland and Ottawa, Ontario

  • Designed lab test environments around the Attero product line
  • Managed technical support and technical sales for the Attero
  • Troubleshot customer issues
  • Directed features development

It was the chance to travel to Scotland that drew me to this opportunity. It was also in my field of Computer Networking - I had a CCNA back then, and I had taken a lot of networking courses in my final year at university. I gained a fair amount of technical knowledge of the IP backhaul of mobile networks. Working with our clients like Cisco and AT&T, I also got to apply the theory of networking like traffic shaping. Unfortunately, the job started to drift more towards sales than I was comfortable with. After leaving the company, I decided to go back to my roots and try my hand at something much more technical.
